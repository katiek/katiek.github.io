Welcome to my portfolio for Harvard's course, DGMD E/S-15 Creative Explorations in Screen-Based and Physical Computing. I am extremely proud of the work I have done in this course and I have been able to a forward to you gaining a greater understanding about what I have been able to gain and accomplish through each instance I have taken this course at Harvard. I also expect my portfolio to serve as an impetus to greater understanding about this very unique course Harvard offers.

...course introduces computing as an expressive medium. We prototype, exhibit, and critique interactive, computational stories, art, and games presented as web applications and physical prototypes.

DGMD E/S-15 Course Website

As a student of Creative Explorations in Screen-Based and Physical Computing, you are put in charge of where your coursework takes you and when one takes this course, you embark on a brand new, immersive study in design, developement and technology. It is impossible to repeat subject matter through taking this course multiple times.

A student's work and studies are based off of the student's current knowledge at the time. Therefore, each successful completion of my studies each instance I took this course, I walked away with a new interactive and deployable web application, gained new and extensive knowledge, and new excitement for furture Digital Media Design work at Harvard and beyond.

"A semester might not seem like a very long time when you want to build something, especially if you don't have much experience using these tools and technologies. It's possible, but it requires that you deeply immerse yourself in the processes of design and development over the four months."

DGMD E/S-15 Course Website

To be successful in this course, a student must be willing to partake in extensive work and study in and out of the physical classroom. The onus is on the student and the student must figure out all the steps necessary in order to produce their own personal final project and presentation.

My portfolio is broken into three parts, one for each semester I took this course. From there you will be able to explore and review my process and projects via my:

  1. General Notes
  2. Coursework
  3. Projects
  4. Next Steps

Thank you in advance for your time and consideration reviewing my portfolio. If you have any questions or would like to speak to me directly, do not hesitate to email me.
